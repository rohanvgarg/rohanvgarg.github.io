Hello! I am currently a Ph.D. student working in the Department of Computer Science at Purdue University. I finished my undergraduate studies in 2020 at The University of Texas at Austin in the wonderful ECE department. My research interests lie in theoretical computer science. In the past, I have enjoyed working on problems in parallel/distributed computing, graph theory, and combinatorial optimization. Recently, I have been interested in algorithmic economics and mechanism design. For more on theory at Purdue, check out the Theory Group Website.

Outside of research, I enjoy playing (rather poorly) Badminton, Soccer, Tennis and Table Tennis. I am an avid fan of the English Premier League and I support Manchester United Football Club. I am almost always listening to music and I enjoy hip-hop, indie/alternative, and some instrumental music. I was born and raised in Austin, Texas, and truly love the city. If we share any interests or you would just like to chat, please do not hesitate to contact me!

I am currently a co-organizer for the Purdue Theory Reading Group. If you are a Purdue student interested in giving a talk on anything math or CS-Theory related, please let me know! I am also a co-organizer for the Purdue Theory Seminar. We have many exciting talks lined up this semester so please join!
I am grateful to have been supported by the Ross Fellowship at Purdue for the 2020-2021 academic year.- "Doctoral applicants to Purdue are considered for the Ross Fellowship, which recognizes academic excellence."
